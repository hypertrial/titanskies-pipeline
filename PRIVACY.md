# Privacy notice

Effective: 2026-07-17.

This notice describes Hypertrial's handling of personal information associated
with the TitanSkies open-source project. It uses British Columbia's
[Personal Information Protection Act](https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/civix/document/id/complete/statreg/00_03063_01)
as its baseline. It is an operational notice, not legal advice.

## Product data

TitanSkies is local-first software. It has no telemetry and sends no user,
credential, warehouse, measurement, geography, or usage data to Hypertrial.
The software makes requests from the operator's environment to NASA Earthdata
and geography providers. Those providers may independently log IP addresses,
account activity, request metadata, or other information under their own
policies.

Do not put personal information, Earthdata credentials, signed URLs, live
NetCDF files, downloaded boundaries, or warehouse content in issues, pull
requests, discussions, logs, or security reports.

## Information Hypertrial may handle

Hypertrial may receive:

- public GitHub account names, commit authorship, DCO sign-offs, reviews,
  issues, and other contribution metadata;
- contact information and message content sent to `team@hypertrial.ai` or
  through private vulnerability reporting; and
- limited service metadata maintained by GitHub or an email provider.

Hypertrial collects, uses, and discloses this information only for reasonable
purposes: operating and securing the project, reviewing and attributing
contributions, responding to requests, enforcing project rules, meeting legal
obligations, and keeping an accurate public record.

## Public contribution record and retention

Git commits, DCO sign-offs, issue and review text, names, account identifiers,
and other voluntarily submitted metadata are public. They may be copied and
retained indefinitely in repository history, mirrors, release archives, and
compliance records. Contributors should use a GitHub-provided no-reply email
address if they do not want a personal email embedded in public commits.

Private correspondence and security reports are retained only as long as
reasonably needed for response, security, legal, accounting, and audit
purposes, then deleted or de-identified where practical. Information may be
retained longer when required by law, needed for legal claims, or already part
of an immutable public contribution record.

## Disclosure, safeguards, and requests

Hypertrial does not sell personal information. It may disclose information to
service providers that host collaboration or communications, professional
advisers, or when authorized or required by law. Public contribution metadata
is disclosed worldwide by design.

Hypertrial uses safeguards appropriate to sensitivity, including
least-privilege access, protected branches, private vulnerability reporting,
secret scanning, and avoiding live data or credentials in CI artifacts. No
system can guarantee absolute security.

To request access to or correction of personal information, withdraw consent
where applicable, or make a complaint, email `team@hypertrial.ai` with enough
detail to locate the record. Hypertrial may verify identity and will respond
within applicable legal timeframes. Public history and legally required
records may not be removable; Hypertrial will explain any refusal.

The designated privacy contact is:

> Privacy Lead, Hypertrial
> `team@hypertrial.ai`

If a concern is not resolved, an individual may contact the Office of the
Information and Privacy Commissioner for British Columbia.

Hypertrial may update this notice as practices or legal requirements change.
The effective date above identifies the current version.
